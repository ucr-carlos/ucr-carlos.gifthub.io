My current professional experience so far consists of working at a concert venue and a retail store. Toward the end of May and start of June of 2022 I started to work at the Hollywood Bowl. I started off as a usher where I would welcome guests and provided seat assistance, creating a warm and organized arrival experience, verify and process tickets for venue entry, maintaining accurate crowd management and high levels of crowd control, provide clear communication to guests regarding event schedules, locations, and upcoming events, and ensure event safety by monitoring patron activity and addressing potential issues, prioritizing security and well-being. I lasted about 3 months in the usher position but then decided to switch over to being a "cushion cashier.

I still worked at the Hollywood Bowl but decided to switch positions because the benefits were sounding better and the job definitely was easier. As a cushion cashier I would efficiently manage large volumes of payments and the distribution of supplies to hundreds of guests while demonstrating excellent customer service in a fast-paced environment, maintain a flawless record of accuracy, processing over 100 transactions per day with 100% accuracy, demonstrating error-free payments and change issuance, monitor concurrent guest count and coordinated transportation to prevent overcrowding and prioritize guest experience, and collaborate with the venue staff to optimize patron seating, ensuring efficient and seamless entry for a smooth audience experience. After the season ended I decided to take a break year of working because it was my final year of High school and I really wanted to enjoy it.
